Introduction

Does your pinky twitch for the semicolon at the end of every sentence? Has 1024 displaced 1000 as a 'round figure' in your head? Do you sincerely believe that "To iterate is human, to recurse divine"? Then Prodaya Codeguru (powered by Codechef) is the place for you to be on April 20th!

Get ready for agonizingly interesting challenges which guarantee to fire your synapses into overdrive.

Pro tip: Ingenuity, quick thinking and resourcefulness will help, as will nimble fingers.

Rules

  • 1. Contest problems will become visible on Sunday, 20th April, 2014 at 10:00 hours.
  • 2. This is a TEAM CONTEST. A team can consist of atmost 2 members. One person can’t be part of more than one team.
  • 3. Cross-college teams are allowed.
  • 4. A team may submit their solutions in C, C++ or JAVA etc. Although different problems can be solved in different languages by the same team.
  • 5. If you do not have a CodeChef ID create one.
  • 6. Please do not discuss strategy, suggestions or tips in the comments during a live contest. Posting questions clarifying the problem statement is ok.
  • 7. Each program will be tested based on our critical test data. However output and input should be exactly as specified in the samples provided.
  • 8. All participants are expected to abide by the CodeChef Code Of Conduct.
  • 9. To be eligible to win prizes, the team members have to be registered on www.prodaya.in.

Judging Criteria

  • 1. All the problems have same points allotted to them.
  • 2. Users are ranked according to the most problems solved. Ties will be broken by the total time for each user in ascending order of time.
  • 3. The decision of the organizers in declaring the results will be final. No queries in this regard will be entertained.
  • 4. Any participant found to be indulging in any form of malpractice will be immediately disqualified.
  • 5. The total time is the sum of the time consumed for each problem solved. The time consumed for a solved problem is the time elapsed from the beginning of the contest to the submission of the first accepted run plus 20 penalty minutes for every previously rejected run for that problem(Wrong answer, Time limit exceeded, Runtime Error, Compilation Error). There is no time consumed for a problem that is not solved.

The violation of above rules may result in team disqualification, the decision to do the same lies in hand of do the same lies in hand of the event coordinators present there. Coordinators reserve the right to change the rules/timings of the contest which will notified to the participants at the latest.

INTRODUCTION


"God used beautiful mathematics in creating the world."- Paul Dirac

Math-o-mania is all about exercising your grey cells and squeezing your brains to solve mathematical questions and puzzles that will test your ingenuity, lateral and creative thinking, cognitive skills and capacity to process information on the fly. The questions will tempt and tease you. At the same time, they will be funny, entertaining and rigorous. You need not to be a Ramanujan to solve these questions. All we are ask you is to come with an open mind and with a passion for juggling numbers.

Rules and Regulations

Team Rules

  • 1. This event is open to all students of undergraduate, postgraduate or equivalent programs.
  • 2. Each team will comprise of TWO students only. Both the students need to be from the same college/institute.
  • 3. After the registration of teams, no change in team structure will be allowed.
  • 4. There is no restriction on the number of teams from an institute.
  • 5. A contestant can be a part of one team only.

Test Rules

  • 1. Each team will solve a single question paper only. Each question paper will be of 60 minutes. Use of calculator is permitted.
  • 2. The questions/puzzles will be of case study type wherein some initial data and situations will be given. The team has to decode this data, draw certain conclusions and infer from the various clues given in each question.
  • 3. The questions will be of Quantitative Type based on high school mathematics syllabus. However, the difficulty and puzzle levels will be quite challenging. Questions will be of mixed type comprising of concepts from Mensuration, Geometry, Permutation & Combination, Data Interpretation, Statistics, Matrix, Algebra, Differential Calculus and Logical Reasoning.
  • 4. Some questions will be of comprehension type wherein the answer of some part will serve as data for the subsequent parts.
  • 5. Use of mobile phones is strictly prohibited. The team members can submit their mobile phone to the coordinators present at the venue and collect them at the end of the test.
  • 6. The teams will be ranked according to their final score. Cash prizes and winning certificates will be given only to the top THREE teams. All contesting teams will receive the participation certificates.
  • 7. Use of any unfair means will lead to immediate disqualification of the team.
  • 8. In case of a tie between two or more teams (by virtue of their score), the teams will be given additional questions to solve. The top three teams will then be ranked according to the minimum time they take to solve these questions.
  • 9. In case of any dispute, the decision of the coordinators will be final and binding on all teams.

The violation of above rules may result in disqualification, the decision to do the same lies in the hand of the judges and event coordinators present there.

Turn Coat Rules:

  • 1. Turn Coat is a Public Speaking competition that judges your ability to effectively speak on two opposites sides of a conflicting topic. You have to support and oppose a topic/theme in the given time.
  • 2. The participants will be provided their respective topics on the spot. The topics will be written on a slip of paper which they'll have to pick on the spot. The participant will start speaking in favour of their topic and the judges shall reward points accordingly. During their speech, the judge will signal them to change their stand on the topic by raising a hand or ringing a bell. Thereafter, the participant will have to speak in opposition of the topic given to them.
  • 3. The time limit for each speaker is 3 minutes. During this period the speaker will have to speak in favour of and oppose the topic by changing his stand (i.e coat) as signalled by the judge. The changeover can be signalled any time by the judge.
  • 4. Each participant will get 2 minutes to prepare his/her speech. Thereafter, he/she shall come on stage immediately and deliver his speech. The time limit for each speaker is 3 minutes.
  • 5. The judges may deduct additional points if the speaker unnecessarily exceeds this time limit. The deduction of points is left to the judges’ discretion. A speaker will be disqualified if he exceeds his time limit by more than 20 seconds.
  • 6. Foul language will not be tolerated. The judges can even disqualify a candidate if he/she is found to use foul language in his debate.
  • 7. The decision of the judges shall be final and binding.
  • 8. Any modifications in the above mentioned rules shall be communicated to the competing teams well in advance. The change in rules shall be final and binding on all the competing teams.
